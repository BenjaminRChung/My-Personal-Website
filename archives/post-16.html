Establishing your baseline.

There's a plethora of 'hacks' to getting more done: gamifying tasks, cold showers, productive tools; the list goes on. I don't doubt their value, but the simplest method I've found is to temper your excitment and establish your baseline.

Your brain will always gravitate towards the most stimulating thing in the room. It's like putting a bowl of brussel sprouts and slice of chocolate cake side by side. I'm going for the cake :)

Appreciate the boredom. Make it so that dreaded task becomes the satisfying stimulant. It's not a means of revolving your life around work, but rather putting greater attention and focus to what matters.
